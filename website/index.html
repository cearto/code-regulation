Coding Gravity

The Tug-of-War between Builders and Breakers

Page Ive, Bharad Raghavan, Amber Rockwood, Cesar Torres

Lessig argues that in order to regulate the Net, we must change its architecture and design. While libertarians such as John Perry Barlow argue that a regulated Net will lose its original anything-goes, laissez-faire flavor, Lessig advocates that these two ideals can co-exist through the careful crafting of code and legislation.

In Code and Other Laws of Cyberspace Lessig discusses regulation on cyber issues that arose during the turn of the century. We will provide an overview of Lessig’s architecture and design principles, as well as a critique of their application to contemporary issues surrounding the Net.

We will explore how issues from the new millenium have evolved as a result of a regulable architecture, or lack thereof, and analyze the strengths and limitations of Lessig’s code-is-god framework.

We will then select emerging issues in the electronic frontier as case studies to which we will apply Lessig’s design principles and examine the tug-of-war between regulation and libertarian ideals.
